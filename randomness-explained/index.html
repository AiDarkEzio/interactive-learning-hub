The 1905 Russian Feud: Setting the Stage

Pavel Nekrasov

"The Tsar of Probability"

Believed that mathematical laws required divine intervention and independence. For Nekrasov, true randomness could only exist through the hand of God, making dependent events impossible to model mathematically.

Andrey Markov

"Andrey The Furious"

Argued that mathematics could model any sequence of events, dependent or independent. His revolutionary idea: the next event depends only on the current state, not the entire history.

Ulam's Impossible Problem

While recovering in the hospital, physicist Stanislaw Ulam wondered about the probability of winning a game of solitaire. The combinations were too vast to calculate directly. How could he find the answer?

Ulam's insight was revolutionary: "Why not just play 100 random games and count the wins?" This is the heart of the Monte Carlo Method. Now, let's apply this to the real, high-stakes problem he faced.
